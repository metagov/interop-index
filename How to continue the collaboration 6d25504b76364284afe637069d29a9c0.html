@November 14, 2024

just a short reflection on what Daniel, Marcin & Jonas talked about, potentially growing to more…

Talking points

  • There are a lot of people that might like to help, but they don’t know where (hard to find, attention gets diverted, so many small to medium size efforts all in their own silo etc)
    • —> Metagov is kind of trying to be a place to direct these people
      • —> We should try to lean into that effort & use it (i.e. channels within metagov, interop things etc)
  • Each project has their own internal goals they’re working towards, and those might not be aligned / lead into all sorts of different directions & priorities
    • —> Would be good to have some goals defined, and then also openly set goals & possibly deadlines etc for individual projects, so that they are motivated (and also have some commitment/pressure) to work into a direction that moves the group/wider goal as a whole forward
  • Many might not have a clear value proposition (as in, confirmed by users etc) or might not be viable in themselves (we should be able to also work on tools that don’t have a business model, as long as it helps us to govern better).
    • However, with a good ecosystem we can create positive feedback loops , such as dogfeeding the products within our circles; finding helpers; getting known and gaining traction; maybe having some other rewards (funding)
  • A lot of things we will need to create good governance & deliberation is interdisciplinary.
    • —> Having a space where ideas can flow and be accessed (i.e. this notion, DataLake, public channels in slack/discord etc…) will be important
      • This however must be handled well, otherwise people will just be lost very fast
      • —> AI can (and probably must) help!
