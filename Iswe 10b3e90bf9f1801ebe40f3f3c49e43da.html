Preamble

The Iswe Community Assembly Platform (better name pending!) can be thought of in two halves: an assembly planning/running tool and a data platform. The former produces data from deliberations (outputs) and the latter ingests it (inputs).

Inputs to the data platform

The same as the outputs.

Outputs from the assembly planning/running tool

  • Deliberative process configuration data as a collection of options and notes
  • An unranked or ranked list with each item containing the following three elements:
    • a note which defines an action selected by the assembly (you can think of this as analogous to e.g. a recommendation from a citizens’ assembly)
    • associated notes and options defining the actors identified as most responsible or well-suited to undertake the action and their 'actor type', respectively
    • a note describing what the members of the assembly think they can do to progress this action (this element TBC)
  • Notes describing updates on progress towards recommended actions (at later dates)
  • Any other notes or documents containing useful information e.g. notetaker notes, transcripts, quotes from participants, multimedia assets such as interviews or illustrations
  • Survey data containing ballots, scored lists and options (TBC), describing aggregated participant demographics, opinion change, and assessment of the process
